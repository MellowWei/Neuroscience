Abstract摘要 · academic submission layer

Problem. Contemporary consciousness neuroscience often moves among several grammars for relating conscious experience to the brain: supervenience, realization, neural correlation, production, and identity. These grammars are not interchangeable. When weaker causal or correlational formulations substitute for identity, the field risks preserving a two-relata structure precisely where it seeks ontological austerity. Approach. This article offers a conceptual and meta-ontological adjudication of that grammar. It grants neuroscience its strongest available ontological formulation — I = brain — then derives a ceiling from the formulation itself: identity is terminal and classificatory, not by itself explanatory. The article then places major theories of consciousness (GNWT/GWT, IIT, HOT, RPT, and PP/FEP) as internally coherent measurement-layer frameworks rather than as mutually exclusive total metaphysics. Contribution. The paper contributes four devices: a cleaned identity formulation, a critique of four weaker stand-ins, the proposed term Neural Co-Position as a replacement for “neural correlate” grammar in boundary work, and a non-adversarial protocol for collaboration between neuroscience and adjacent first-person, phenomenological, and conceptual disciplines.

问题。当代意识神经科学经常在几种语法之间移动：随附、实现、神经相关、生成、同一。这些语法不可互换。当较弱的因果或相关表述替代同一命题时，学科会在追求本体论简洁的同时，暗中保留两个相关项的结构。方法。本文对这种语法进行概念分析与元本体论裁决：先授予神经科学其最强可用本体论表述——我 = 大脑——再从该命题自身导出射程上限：同一是终结性、分类性的，并不自动构成解释。随后，本文将 GNWT/GWT、IIT、HOT、RPT、PP/FEP 等主流意识理论放置为各自测量层内自洽的框架，而不是彼此互斥的总体形而上学。贡献。本文贡献四项装置：干净的同一命题表述；对四个较弱替身的批判；以 Neural Co-Position / 神经共位 替代 “neural correlate / 神经相关物” 语法的术语提案；以及神经科学与第一人称、现象学、概念分析等相邻学科之间的非对抗合作协议。

— §1 — Four Weak Formulations · 四种较弱表述 —
同一关系的四个替身机制Four Stand-Ins for the Identity Relation

Four weaker formulations regularly stand in for identity and silently retain dualism. The identity formulation is the only one that does not presuppose what neuroscience is meant to be eliminating.

四种较弱表述常常替代同一命题，并暗中保留二元论。同一表述是唯一一个不预设神经科学本应消解之物的命题表述。

I Stand-In Formulation

Supervenience随附性

心理性质随附于物理性质。Mental properties supervene on physical properties.

Defect. Too weak — permits multiple realization and leaves room for non-physical addenda. Allows the door of dualism to remain ajar by giving mental properties their own existence-status alongside physical ones.

Submission-layer argument. Supervenience secures dependence without identity: no mental difference without some physical difference, but not necessarily mental = neural. This makes it useful as a restraint on dualism, yet insufficient as the strongest neuroscientific claim.
II Stand-In Formulation

Realization实现

心理状态被神经状态实现。Mental states are realized by neural states.

Defect. "Realization" presupposes a distinction between the realized and the realizer. The very grammar of the verb encodes two-thing ontology before the metaphysics has been argued.

Submission-layer argument. Realization language is powerful for functionalism and multiple realizability, but it keeps a role/filler distinction. N-B-A accepts that distinction as useful for modeling while refusing it as the terminal ontology of the I.
III Stand-In Formulation

Neural Correlate of Consciousness · NCC意识的神经相关物

每一意识状态对应某种神经模式。Each conscious state corresponds to some neural pattern.

Defect. Crypto-dualist. "Correlate" presupposes two relata: the methodological language of the discipline silently maintains the dualism the discipline officially rejects. Replaced in §5 by Neural Co-Position.

Submission-layer argument. NCC remains experimentally indispensable as a search program, but its wording frames consciousness and neural activity as paired items. Neural Co-Position preserves empirical mapping while removing the implication that two ontologically separate relata must be correlated.
IV Stand-In Formulation

Production生成

大脑生成意识。The brain produces consciousness.

Defect. Smuggles a causal asymmetry: A produces B presupposes A ≠ B. The strongest form of dualism re-entry through the back door of causal verbs.

Submission-layer argument. Production is a useful dynamic description when modeling transitions into reportability, access, or content stabilization. It becomes metaphysically misleading only when promoted from process-description to ontology-description.
The four stand-ins together = the un-stated dualism of contemporary neuroscience. 四个替身合起来 = 当代神经科学未明言的二元论。

It is a question about whether the description-language of neuroscience can saturate the experiential domain it now claims to be identical with. This question is not internal to the identity claim; it sits on its boundary. N-B-A does not ask neuroscience to solve the Hard Problem. It asks neuroscience to acknowledge that the Hard Problem lies outside its ontological reach, and to return that problem to the adjacent disciplines that handle inter-layer placement language. This is a generosity, not a diminishment.
