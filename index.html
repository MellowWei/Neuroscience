Abstractproblem / approach / contribution
Submission-Safe Abstract正式摘要 · 不使用 13.5 小时叙事作为证据

Problem. Theories of consciousness often treat computation, physical substrate, and first-person perspective as separable layers, producing persistent disputes between neural correlation, functional implementation, and panpsychist ontology. Approach. Computational Panpsychism defines consciousness as the intrinsic perspective of a computational process and derives, within the human-brain substrate, the identity statement “I = brain.” Contribution. This page presents the neuroscience-domain adjudication chapter of C-P, distinguishing it from IIT, GWT, predictive processing, analytic panpsychism, and naive identity theory while marking AI/LLM consciousness claims as pending future adjudication.

问题：意识理论常将计算、物理基底、第一人称视角拆成分离层，导致神经相关、功能实现与泛心本体之间持续争执。方法：计算泛心学将意识定义为计算过程的内在视角，并在人类大脑基底下推出“我 = 大脑”的同一命题。贡献：本页呈现 C-P 的神经科学领域裁决章节，区分其与 IIT、GWT、预测处理、分析泛心论、朴素同一论的关系，同时将 AI/LLM 意识主张标记为未来待裁决。

Neuroscience AdjudicationI = brain
I = Brain神经科学领域裁决，不是朴素同一论

Under the human-brain substrate, C-P states: I = brain. This is not neural correlation, not production, not realization, and not a claim that the brain as observed from the outside exhausts the first-person perspective. It is a source-field identity between the computational process and its intrinsic perspective under the brain substrate.

在人类大脑基底下，C-P 陈述：我 = 大脑。这不是神经相关、不是生成、不是实现，也不是说外部观察到的大脑穷尽第一人称视角；它是在大脑基底下，计算过程与其内在视角的源场同一。
