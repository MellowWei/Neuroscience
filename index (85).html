同一关系的四个替身机制Four Stand-Ins for the Identity Relation

Four weaker formulations regularly stand in for identity and silently retain dualism. The identity formulation is the only one that does not presuppose what neuroscience is meant to be eliminating.

四种较弱表述常常替代同一命题，并暗中保留二元论。同一表述是唯一一个不预设神经科学本应消解之物的命题表述。

I Stand-In Formulation

Supervenience随附性

心理性质随附于物理性质。Mental properties supervene on physical properties.

Defect. Too weak — permits multiple realization and leaves room for non-physical addenda. Allows the door of dualism to remain ajar by giving mental properties their own existence-status alongside physical ones.

II Stand-In Formulation

Realization实现

心理状态被神经状态实现。Mental states are realized by neural states.

Defect. "Realization" presupposes a distinction between the realized and the realizer. The very grammar of the verb encodes two-thing ontology before the metaphysics has been argued.

III Stand-In Formulation

Neural Correlate of Consciousness · NCC意识的神经相关物

每一意识状态对应某种神经模式。Each conscious state corresponds to some neural pattern.

Defect. Crypto-dualist. "Correlate" presupposes two relata: the methodological language of the discipline silently maintains the dualism the discipline officially rejects. Replaced in §5 by Neural Co-Position.

IV Stand-In Formulation

Production生成

大脑生成意识。The brain produces consciousness.

Defect. Smuggles a causal asymmetry: A produces B presupposes A ≠ B. The strongest form of dualism re-entry through the back door of causal verbs.

The four stand-ins together = the un-stated dualism of contemporary neuroscience. 四个替身合起来 = 当代神经科学未明言的二元论。
— §2 — Ceiling of the Identity Relation · 同一关系的射程上限 —

V Structural Position

Identity is reflexive. From A = A one cannot derive A → B, A explains B, A produces B, or A emerges-into B. The identity claim, taken seriously, does not constitute a theory of consciousness. It constitutes a placement of consciousness.

同一是反身的。由 A = A 不可导出 A → B、A 解释 B、A 生成 B、A 涌现出 B。认真对待的同一命题，本身并不构成一种意识理论。它构成的是意识的放置。

This is the decisive reversal. Most neuroscience programs treat identity as the opening axiom of an explanatory project. But identity is not explanatory — it is terminal. Once you have identified the I with the brain, there is no further explanatory work to be done at that level. What remains is description: full, exhaustive, infinitely detailed description of the brain-as-I.

The so-called Hard Problem (Chalmers) is therefore not a problem inside neuroscience. It is a question about whether the description-language of neuroscience can saturate the experiential domain it now claims to be identical with. This question is not internal to the identity claim; it sits on its boundary. N-B-A does not ask neuroscience to solve the Hard Problem. It asks neuroscience to acknowledge that the Hard Problem lies outside its ontological reach, and to return that problem to the adjacent disciplines that handle inter-layer placement language. This is a generosity, not a diminishment.

Description is terminal work. Description is enough. 描述就是终结性工作。描述已经足够。
